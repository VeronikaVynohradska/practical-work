Erasmus project in Lithuania

Now you're probably wondering why I decided to make it separate page.


It's special

Everything there was perfect from the acommodation to the idea of the project and activities we were doing during it

  1. The aim of the porject was to create a documentary about Kamariskes manor, recording and editing sounds and videos.
  2. During this week we got closer to the world of the video and music editing.
  3. Also learnt a lot about the cultural heritage and the nature that surround the manor.
  4. "The participants will be host in our amazing 17th century monastery, located in the village of Antaliepte, an inspiring and historical place where young people will live and spending time together"

I shared room with two other Ukrainian girls and we found a perfect connection. The whole our team was very active, Ukrainians tried everything available on a project.

Kayaking or lake before breakfast? Yes!

Go to Kamariskes extra time? Yes!

The longest reflections? Of course Ukrainians!

Working with professional camera and light? Nata, let's stay a bit more, pleeeease, thx-thx)

Dancing during each cultural night till the end? Also about our team!


From my Facebook post:

"These days were full of positive moments, dances, conversations, games, and, of course, work: we wrote scripts, filmed, edited, recorded voiceovers, acted, and, most importantly, worked as a team. So enjoy these photos where we are free, full (maybe not always, but nevertheless) of energy and life. p.s. I constantly caught myself with the "look" of the camera, and I probably received as many compliments in these 10 days as I have in my entire life!"
